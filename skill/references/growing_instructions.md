# Detailed Growing Instructions by Variety

## Universal Principles

**Two Growing Methods:**
- **Jar Sprouting** — Best for: mung beans, alfalfa, lentils, broccoli sprouts. Minimal equipment, no soil, fastest.
- **Tray Growing (Microgreens)** — Best for: sunflower, pea shoots, radish, kale, beet, red cabbage, buckwheat. Requires tray + growing medium. More yield, better nutrition.

**The Blackout Trick:** Most microgreens benefit from 3-5 days of darkness after seeding (covered tray). This stretches the hypocotyl (stem), making greens taller and more tender — a technique used by commercial growers.

**Bottom Watering:** Once uncovered, water from below by pouring into the outer tray. This prevents mold on the growing medium surface and encourages roots to grow downward.

## BROCCOLI

### As Sprouts (Jar Method)
**Best for:** Maximum sulforaphane (anti-inflammatory, cancer prevention, detox)

**Equipment:** Mason jar, sprouting lid or cheesecloth + rubber band

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0, Evening  → Measure 2 tbsp seeds. Rinse thoroughly. Soak in jar with 1 cup water (8-12 hrs).
Day 1, Morning  → Drain completely. Invert jar at 45° angle in a bowl (allows air + drainage).
                   Keep in dark or indirect light. Rinse & drain every 12 hours.
Day 2           → Small white tails should be visible. Continue rinsing twice daily.
Day 3           → Sprouts 0.5-1 inch long. Move to indirect light briefly to develop chlorophyll (optional).
Day 3-4         → HARVEST WINDOW: Tails are ½–1 inch; flavor is mild, sulforaphane is at peak.
```

**Seeding density:** 2 tablespoons yields approx. 1-2 cups of sprouts
**Temperature:** 65-75°F (18-24°C) ideal
**Rinse water:** Use filtered or room-temperature water
**Harvest cue:** White tails ½–1 inch long. Hulls may still be attached — that's fine.
**Storage:** Refrigerate in jar with loose lid; use within 5-7 days. Rinse before eating.
**Food safety note:** Always use clean equipment; rinse at least 2x per day to prevent bacteria.

### As Microgreens (Tray Method)
**Best for:** Vitamins C, K, E + antioxidants; slightly lower sulforaphane than sprouts

**Equipment:** 10x20 tray with holes, 10x20 solid tray, growing medium (soil or coco coir), spray bottle

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak seeds 4-6 hrs. Fill tray with 1-1.5 inches moistened soil/coco coir.
                   Spread seeds evenly (about 1 oz/10x20 tray). Mist seeds lightly.
Day 1-4         → Cover with humidity dome or another tray (BLACKOUT PHASE).
                   Mist lightly if medium feels dry. Do NOT overwater.
Day 4-5         → Lift cover: sprouts 1-2 inches tall with yellow/white stems.
                   Move to light. Begin bottom-watering.
Day 5-7         → Greening phase. Stems turn green; cotyledons open.
                   Water from below once daily or when top inch of medium feels dry.
Day 8-12        → HARVEST WINDOW: Cotyledons fully open, 2-3 inches tall, vibrant green.
```

**Seeding density:** 1 oz (28g) per 10x20 tray
**Light:** 12-16 hrs/day of natural light or grow light (LED, 2-4 inches above)
**Temperature:** 60-75°F (16-24°C)
**Harvest cue:** Cotyledons open and bright green; first true leaves may just be emerging
**How to harvest:** Cut with clean scissors just above soil line
**Storage:** Rinse, spin dry or pat dry, store in airtight container with paper towel in refrigerator. Lasts 5-7 days.

## RADISH

**Best for:** Digestion, general nutrition, beginners wanting fast results
**Form:** Microgreen (also works as sprout)
**Harvest time:** 5-7 days (one of the fastest microgreens)

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak seeds 4-6 hrs. Spread on moistened medium (dense—radish seeds are small).
                   About 1.5 oz per 10x20 tray. Mist thoroughly.
Day 1-3         → BLACKOUT: Cover. Mist surface lightly once daily if dry.
Day 3-4         → Uncover: 1-2 inch stems visible. Move to light.
Day 4-5         → Greening phase. Water from below.
Day 5-7         → HARVEST: Cotyledons open, bright green with purple-tinged stems.
                   Flavor is spicy and peppery.
```

**Seeding density:** 1.5 oz per 10x20 tray
**Light:** 12-16 hrs/day
**Temperature:** 60-75°F
**Flavor:** Spicy/peppery. Milder if harvested earlier; more intense if left longer.
**Troubleshooting:** Radish is prone to damping off (mold) if overwatered — ensure good airflow.

## SUNFLOWER

**Best for:** Energy, complete protein, general nutrition
**Form:** Microgreen
**Harvest time:** 10-14 days
**Note:** Use unhulled, raw sunflower seeds. Hulled seeds mold more easily.

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak unhulled seeds 8-12 hrs (important—they're large).
                   Drain & let rest 8 more hrs. Some tails may start showing.
Day 1           → Fill tray with 1.5-2 inches of soil/coco coir.
                   Spread seeds in a single tight layer (about 2 oz per 10x20 tray).
                   Mist generously. Cover with weighted tray on top.
Day 1-5         → BLACKOUT PHASE with weight on top (the weight helps seeds shed their hulls).
                   Check moisture daily — sunflower needs consistent moisture at this stage.
Day 5-6         → Remove cover. Stems should be 2-4 inches, pushing hulls off.
                   Move to light. Manually remove stubborn hulls.
Day 6-10        → GREENING: Water from below. Watch for large, paddle-like cotyledons opening.
Day 10-14       → HARVEST: Cotyledons fully open, 4-6 inches tall. Cut above soil.
```

**Seeding density:** 2 oz per 10x20 tray (dense single layer)
**Light:** 14-16 hrs/day; bright light is important for sunflower
**Temperature:** 65-75°F
**Common issue:** Hull removal — if hulls don't come off, mist them and wait; weighted blackout helps
**Harvest cue:** Large yellow-green paddle leaves fully spread open
**Flavor:** Mild, nutty, slightly sweet

## PEA SHOOTS

**Best for:** Energy, general nutrition, beginners, kids (mild sweet flavor)
**Form:** Microgreen
**Harvest time:** 8-12 days

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak peas 8-12 hrs (they are large seeds, need longer soak).
                   Fill tray with 1.5-2 inches of soil. Spread in a single layer (dense).
Day 1-4         → BLACKOUT. Mist if dry. Peas germinate quickly — expect growth by day 2.
Day 4-5         → Uncover. Stems 3-5 inches tall. Move to bright light.
Day 5-8         → Water from below once daily. Stems will continue elongating.
Day 8-12        → HARVEST: When stems have 2-3 sets of leaves and tendrils appear.
                   Harvest the top 3-4 inches for best flavor (leave base for possible regrowth).
```

**Seeding density:** 2-3 oz per 10x20 tray (seeds are large)
**Light:** 12-16 hrs/day
**Temperature:** 55-70°F — peas prefer slightly cooler temperatures
**Tip:** Pea shoots can sometimes be harvested twice (cut above growing point for regrowth)
**Flavor:** Sweet, green-pea flavor — the most palatable for picky eaters
**Note:** Grow in soil rather than hydroponically — peas need the root support

## MUNG BEAN SPROUTS

**Best for:** Digestion, beginner sprout growers, Asian cuisine
**Form:** Jar sprout
**Harvest time:** 3-5 days

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0, Evening  → Rinse 1/4 cup mung beans. Soak 8-12 hrs in jar.
Day 1, Morning  → Drain. Invert at 45°. Keep warm (they like warmth — 70-80°F ideal).
Day 1-2         → Rinse and drain every 8-12 hrs. Place in dark warm spot.
Day 2-3         → White tails 1-2 inches long. Getting close!
Day 3-5         → HARVEST: Tails are 1-3 inches.
                   Optional: briefly expose to light for slight greening of tips (optional).
```

**Yield:** 1/4 cup beans → approx 2 cups sprouts
**Temperature:** 68-80°F — warmth speeds germination
**Rinse frequency:** Every 8-12 hrs is ideal; twice daily is minimum
**Flavor:** Mild, crunchy, classic bean sprout flavor
**Storage:** Refrigerate with loose lid; use within 3-4 days
**Note:** Best eaten raw or briefly stir-fried to preserve enzymes

## KALE

**Best for:** Immune support, bone health, weight management
**Form:** Microgreen
**Harvest time:** 8-12 days

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak 4-6 hrs optional (speeds germination).
                   Fill tray with 1 inch growing medium.
                   Spread seeds evenly (1 oz per 10x20 tray). Mist.
Day 1-4         → BLACKOUT. Mist lightly once daily. Kale germinates fast.
Day 4-5         → Uncover. Move to light. Small curved stems unfurling.
Day 5-8         → Greening phase. Water from below.
Day 8-12        → HARVEST: Cotyledons open, deep green, 2-3 inches tall.
```

**Seeding density:** 1 oz per 10x20 tray
**Light:** 12-16 hrs/day
**Temperature:** 60-75°F
**Flavor:** Mildly bitter, earthy, like young kale
**Tip:** Red Russian kale has more tender leaves and a slightly sweeter taste than curly kale

## ALFALFA SPROUTS

**Best for:** Immune support, bone health, beginners wanting classic sprouts
**Form:** Jar sprout
**Harvest time:** 5-6 days

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0, Evening  → Rinse 2 tbsp seeds. Soak 4-6 hrs (don't oversoak — seeds are tiny).
Day 1           → Drain. Rinse thoroughly. Invert jar at angle in bowl. Keep in dim light.
Day 1-5         → Rinse and drain 2x per day (morning + evening).
Day 5           → Move jar to indirect light for 4-8 hrs to green up the leaves.
Day 5-6         → HARVEST: Bright green, fluffy, 1-2 inches long.
```

**Yield:** 2 tbsp seeds → 2 cups of sprouts
**Temperature:** 65-75°F
**Key tip:** Alfalfa seeds are very small — use a fine mesh sprouting lid or layer of cheesecloth
**Storage:** Refrigerate loosely; use within 5-7 days
**Flavor:** Mild, fresh, grassy — the classic sandwich sprout

## RED CABBAGE

**Best for:** Anti-inflammatory, cancer prevention, antioxidants
**Form:** Microgreen
**Harvest time:** 8-12 days

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak seeds 4-6 hrs. Fill tray. Seed at 1 oz per 10x20 tray. Mist.
Day 1-4         → BLACKOUT. Check moisture daily.
Day 4-5         → Uncover. Note stunning purple-magenta stems.
Day 5-8         → Greening phase. The cotyledons will be green on top with purple underside.
Day 8-12        → HARVEST: Cotyledons open, vivid colors visible, 2-3 inches.
```

**Flavor:** Mild cabbage flavor, slightly peppery
**Visual appeal:** Purple stems make this stunning in salads
**Nutrition note:** Red/purple color = anthocyanins = one of the highest antioxidant microgreens available

## BUCKWHEAT

**Best for:** Heart health, energy, general nutrition (good for those avoiding gluten)
**Form:** Microgreen
**Harvest time:** 8-12 days
**Note:** Use raw, unhulled buckwheat groats

**Day-by-Day Timeline:**
```
Day 0           → Soak 8 hrs. Rinse well (buckwheat releases mucilage/sliminess — rinse thoroughly!).
Day 1           → Fill tray with 1.5 inches soil. Spread in loose single layer. Mist.
Day 1-5         → BLACKOUT. Bottom water from day 2 onward.
Day 5-6         → Uncover. Distinctive heart-shaped cotyledons forming.
Day 6-8         → Greening phase. Move to light.
Day 8-12        → HARVEST: Heart-shaped cotyledons bright green, 2-4 inches tall.
```

**Flavor:** Mild, earthy, slightly nutty
**Important:** Hulls will be present — remove during harvest or rinse well; most are edible
**Seeding density:** 1.5 oz per 10x20 tray

## Equipment Guide

### Minimal Budget Setup (~$15-30)
- Any shallow container 2-3 inches deep (repurpose plastic food containers — poke drainage holes)
- 1 spray bottle ($3-5)
- Potting mix or coconut coir ($5-10)
- Mason jar + cheesecloth for sprouts ($3-5)
- Place near a south-facing window
